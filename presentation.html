02 / Research

Research

"Founding and consolidating NLP at Uniandes"

NLP research line

The first NLP research line in the department — built from zero

When Rubén joined the department in August 2021, no NLP faculty existed at Uniandes. Drawing on his doctoral research and industry experience in AI, he established the first dedicated NLP research line within the FlagLab group—a line that had been considered a risky bet before large language models transformed the field.

Today, the group is recognized nationally and has achieved visibility at international venues including NeurIPS, AAAI, and EMNLP.

2021 "No NLP faculty at Uniandes — line established"

Research highlight

Wayuunaiki, Arhuaco, Inga, Nasa — Colombia's first neural MT models

This project develops machine translation systems for four low-resource Colombian indigenous languages using Transformer-based architectures and transfer learning. By constructing open parallel corpora, it advances the digital preservation of communities whose languages are considered endangered.

Project website: colombialanguages.virtual.uniandes.edu.co

AAAI 2026 Improving Low-Resource Translation with Dictionary-Guided Fine-Tuning and RL: A Spanish-to-Wayuunaiki Study
NAACL 2024 Translation systems for Colombian Indigenous languages — first step towards cultural preservation
SN Comput. Sci. 2025 Machine Translation Strategies for Low-Resource Colombian Indigenous Languages

Research highlights

Medical education and historical linguistics — NLP beyond engineering

Virtual Patient System
LLM-powered virtual patient for clinical anamnesis training—supporting Colombian medical students with adaptive, realistic patient dialogues and immediate feedback. Built in collaboration with University of Luxembourg and the Uniandes School of Medicine.
CSBJ 2025 (Q1) TSAR @ EMNLP 2025 (A*) Collab: U. Luxembourg · School of Medicine
Historical Ink
Computational analysis of 19th-century Latin American Spanish newspapers: semantic shift detection, irony identification, and gender semantics through NLP methods applied to historical corpora. Interdisciplinary collaboration with the Department of History (Prof. Jaime Borja).
EMNLP 2024 (A*) NLP4DH @ NAACL 2025 (A) Collab: Dept. of History · Jaime Borja
