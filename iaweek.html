About the event

Accelerating AI talent across Latin America

AI Week aims to foster talent development, accelerate AI adoption, and strengthen collaboration between academia and industry. Participants gain skills in Agentic AI, Generative AI, and High-Performance Computing (HPC), positioning Colombia at the forefront of digital transformation and competitiveness across Latin America.

Held in partnership with NVIDIA, the event delivers certification-oriented Deep Learning Institute (DLI) workshops using NVIDIA-provided infrastructure. More than 21 national and international speakers participate, including NVIDIA ambassadors from Mexico and Peru who serve as instructors.

AI Week also functions as a direct talent pipeline into graduate NLP research programs. Many of the master's students at Uniandes have noted they discovered the program through AI Week or the NVIDIA workshops, making the event a cornerstone for attracting the next generation of AI researchers.

(2024 → 2025)

2024 — The foundation

829 participants · 206 NVIDIA certifications

The first edition attracted 829 participants, the majority undergraduate students from Colombian universities. Uniandes students accounted for 47% of total attendance.

206 NVIDIA certifications in Artificial Intelligence and Generative AI were awarded, with an estimated total value of USD 103,000.

2025 — Regional scale

1,429 participants · 96 universities · 425 certifications

Significantly expanded scope. 1,429 undergraduate and graduate students participated from 96 universities across Latin America, predominantly from Colombia.

425 NVIDIA certifications were awarded — an estimated value of USD 232,500. The official 2025 report is available at ingenieria.uniandes.edu.co/es/AI-Week-2025.

AI Week 2025 · Supported by AWS

First Generative AI Hackathon in Uniandes

In the 2025 edition, we organized the first Generative AI Hackathon in Uniandes, focused on health-related AI applications. More than 20 teams participated, with five selected as finalists and three as final winners.

AWS provided both computational resources and technical staff who supported participants throughout the event. As part of the challenge, teams developed disease diagnosis assistants using existing large language models.
